SITE PREPARATION
It will be necessary to remove some of the localised surface vegetation and trees to facilitate the preparation of a building platform, which will be performed in accordance with Lake Macquarie Council DCP1 (Revision 01) - 2.1.4 “Tree Preservation and Management” restrictions.
Tree protection measures are to be installed to Street trees prior to commencement of site preparation works where required, in accordance with Council’s Tree Preservation requirements.
The site preparation works are to be performed in accordance with Lake Macquarie City Council DCP1 (Revision 01) - 2.1.9 "Sloping Land & Soils" requirements, with excavation and fill batters to be stabilised by turf or suitably designed and constructed retaining walls where required. Plans and specifications endorsed by a suitably qualified Structural Engineer are to be submitted to Council for approval where excavated and filled areas exceed 1m in height prior to construction of retaining wall structures if necessary.
Appropriate and compliant Silt Fabric Fencing and trenches will be provided and maintained throughout the course of construction in accordance with Lake Macquarie City Council DCP1 (Revision 01) - 2.1.11 "Erosion Prevention and Sediment Control", to minimise run-off from the building site.
An all-weather access point to the building site is to be constructed prior to commencement of construction works to enable materials to be tracked onto the building site, in accordance with Lake Macquarie City Council DCP1 (Revision 01) - 2.1.11 "Erosion Prevention and Sediment Control".
Waste containment is to be by means of a Council compliant waste containment facility to be constructed and maintained on site.
Upon completion of the dwelling it is anticipated that the future occupants will undertake to plant replacement indigenous Australian trees and shrubs in and around the immediate vicinity of the dwelling for both aesthetic and privacy reasons, and to attract and create a good environment for native bird life.
There will be no additional pollution caused by the erection of the new dwelling.

UTILITY SERVICES
Power is to be provided to the dwelling by means of an underground connection from the service main located at the front boundary.
Water supply will be adequate by means of connection into the water main located at the front boundary and augmented by connection to an on-site rainwater collection tank.
Storm water from the dwelling will be directed initially to a 5000 litre Poly Above ground water tank positioned towards the rear of the dwelling, with overflow from the tank to be directed via PVC drainage lines to the street.
Water from the collection tank is to be connected to the dwelling’s WC’s, washing machine and external yard taps, in accordance with BASIX requirements.
Accumulated surface water is to be directed to the street via independent PVC drainage lines where necessary.
Sewerage disposal from the dwelling is to be directed to the an Aerobic Septic System, approval of which is sought under a separate application to Council.
There will be no change to the natural environment, the built environment, or items of natural heritage.
GARAGE, SHEDS AND DRIVEWAY ACCESS
It can be noted from the accompanying plans that the dwelling will incorporate an attached double car garage, and that adequate parking for vehicles is provided in front of the garage and wholly within the property boundary.
Access to the site by heavy vehicles for the purposes of delivering building materials will be by means of the sealed public access, Cleveland St. Access to and from the site will not impede traffic flow on the dedicated Public Rd.
It can be concluded that the above-mentioned will satisfy the objectives of the DCP 1 (Revision 01) – 3.4.8 "Garage Sheds and Driveway Access"
